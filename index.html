System Diagnostics Glossary Reference

Click any section header below to expand technical parameters and baseline metrics.

Yellow LED High HR Trigger

Lights up instantly when the calculated heart rate matches or crosses above 100 BPM. Flags potential tachycardia states or heavy cardiovascular exertion.

Blue LED Display Mode Toggle Status

Indicates which biometric value is actively being pushed to the physical screen on the hardware module. When lit, the device shows SpO2; when dark, it tracks Heart Rate.

Red LED Signal Quality Error Fault

Lights up when finger contact is insecure. Activated if the status code drops below 3 and tracking confidence dips below 95%.

Status 3 (Fully Operational): Optical channels securely locked onto arterial pulse flow.
Status 2 (Acquiring Vitals Pulse): Scanner searching for rhythmic spikes to calibrate standard reading data.
Status 1 (Poor Alignment / High Noise): Reading drift caused by excessive light leaks or sudden user movement.
Status 0 (No Contact Detected): Absolute dropout. Finger completely detached from photodiode interface.
Alert Condition Triggers Bounds Clinical Priority Context Explanations
High Heart Rate => 100 BPM Flags tachycardia. Can be caused by exercise, anxiety, fever, or stress.
Low Heart Rate < 60 BPM Flags bradycardia. Normal in well-trained resting athletes, but requires evaluation if paired with dizziness.
Hypoxic Condition < 95% Low blood oxygen saturation indicates inadequate cell ventilation. Requires prompt attention.

Dynamic Exercise Target Zones During vigorous cardiovascular movement, a person's safe target heart rate typically scales up to 60% to 85% of their theoretical maximum heart rate (calculated roughly as 220 - age). SpO2 metrics should stay steady above 95% during healthy exertion; dynamic drops highlight lower physical conditioning.
Severity Scale & When to Worry
Mild Variance: Heart rate 101-110 (at rest, could be due to caffeine or mild stress); SpO2 at 93%-94% (monitor closely, limit movement exertion).
Severe / Critical: Resting heart rate persistently above 120 or below 50 accompanied by fainting/fatigue. SpO2 below 92% is a direct critical danger signal requiring immediate evaluation.
